About Me

Hey! My name is Bas Châtel, and you must be here to check out my personal site! This site is made partially for me to practice some site development but mainly to let you get to know me a bit. What I’ve been up to lately etc. So first let me tell you a little bit about myself.

I am a 24 year old student at the University of Amsterdam who has just finished his bachelor in Psychobiology and a minor in Programming. I know, I know.. Quite a big change to go from studying the brain (from a molecular to psychological level) to begin learning how to program stuff. Or at least that’s how it felt to me in the beginning. But programming is becoming an increasingly important skill (with all the big data, systems neuroscience and what not) that’s quite handy for visualising and analysing just about anything you want! That’s one of the main reasons I enrolled in my latest project, the pre-master of Medical Informatics (pretty excited about that one to be honest). If you want to know more about these educational programs I’ve been following, just click on those links and it will take you to everything you need to know!

As you can see underneath this section there is a portfolio of all kinds of projects I’ve been doing. This ranges from my bachelor thesis to all kinds of programming projects. This will grow over time and more projects will be added! Please take a look around and try some stuff out. And if you enjoy it, have some questions, pointers for improvement or suggestions (maybe for a next project or something) you can always contact me via the contact form at the bottom of the page, and I will answer as soon as possible. Have fun! (oh and if you want to have a peek at the codes from the projects, check my Github!)
